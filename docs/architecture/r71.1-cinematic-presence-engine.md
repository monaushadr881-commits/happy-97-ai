# R71.1 — HAPPY Cinematic Presence & VFX Engine

Expansion-only. Sits on top of R71 CLDHE. Adds VFX, particle, smoke,
lighting, camera, and quality-tier engines without touching business logic
or the existing Digital Human identity.

## VFX architecture

```
HappyStage
 ├─ LightingLayer   (ambient, key, rim, ground bloom)
 ├─ SmokeLayer      (volumetric, ground-hugging, capped opacity)
 ├─ ParticleLayer   (dust, gold sparks, blue AI, fireflies)
 ├─ GroundLayer     (ripple, energy ring, footstep response)
 ├─ CharacterLayer  (avatar, walking, idle, lip sync)
 └─ CameraLayer     (micro-motion, focus shift, DoF)
```

Every layer respects the active `QualityTier` (ultra/high/medium/low/auto)
and the user's `prefers-reduced-motion` setting.

## Particle engine

Pooled instanced sprites. Never allocates during a frame. Emits dust,
golden sparks, blue holographic particles, ambient fireflies, and tiny
energy fragments. Hard cap: 512 particles on ultra, 64 on low.

## Smoke engine

Ground-hugging volumetric smoke rendered with a screen-space shader.
Constraints:
- opacity clamp ≤ 0.25
- vertical extent ≤ 30% of viewport
- never over primary UI text nodes
- automatically disabled on `low` tier and reduced-motion

## Lighting

Dynamic ambient light, soft character rim glow, adaptive environment
sampling from the surrounding UI palette (uses existing design tokens),
natural contact shadow under the avatar.

## Animation pipeline

- Idle: blink, breathing, weight shift, finger micro-motion
- Walking: weight shift, foot placement, shoulder / arm swing
- Listening: attentive pose, small nod, eye tracking to caret / cursor
- Thinking: only when real work is in flight
- Speaking: viseme + emotion blend
- Exit: smile → turn → walk back → particles fade → smoke dissolves → dock

## Performance strategy

- 60 fps target, `requestAnimationFrame`, GPU-composited layers only
- LOD by distance and quality tier
- Frustum culling for particles
- Shared textures + animation caches
- Zero CLS: stage is a fixed-size portal, never reflows page content

## Accessibility strategy

`prefers-reduced-motion` disables walking cinematics, camera motion,
smoke, and heavy particle bursts. All greetings, listening, and speaking
states remain fully accessible via text + ARIA live regions.
